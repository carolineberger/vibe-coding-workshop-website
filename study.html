Vibe Coding: Ocean Science Computational Environments

About the Study

The aim of this study is to explore the programming environments, tools, and workflows used by ocean scientists. Your participation will help identify design requirements for programming environments for ocean scientists authoring interactive elements.

During the vibe coding workshop, participants can opt-in to sharing their programming and vibe coding activity with the research team. The participants' code snippets and chats with the LLM (e.g., Claude or Copilot) will be logged and stored in a secure location only accessible by members of the research team. To protect the privacy of participants' research, domain specific information will be shared only with members of the research team and will be removed prior to publication.

Your participation is voluntary. This study is conducted by researchers at Aarhus University. For questions, please contact Caroline Berger at caroline.berger@cs.au.dk and +4561474350.

About the Research Team

The research team belongs to the Human-Centered Computing section at Aarhus University in Denmark. The group explores how digital technologies can seamlessly integrate into human activities, enhancing collaboration, creativity, and decision-making. By combining expertise in human-computer interaction, ubiquitous computing, and participatory design, the group investigates how computing systems can be made more intuitive, adaptive, and accessible. We study how people interact with digital tools in work and everyday life, developing new interaction techniques, software platforms, and programming environments that respond to human needs.
